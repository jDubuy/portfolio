Internship Context

I completed this 10-week internship in the packaging department of the ALS (Aquitaine Légumes Surgelés) site of the Ardo group, a major European player in frozen vegetables. The site handles the final packaging of vegetables for major brands (Picard, Findus, Lidl...) using 3 production lines (lines 1 & 2 for bags from 400g to 2.7kg, line 3 for bulk mixtures and bags of 10kg+).

My industrial tutor, Olivier Mas, entrusted me with the cross-cutting modernization of the department's decision-support tools.

🎯

Main Mission

The central objective was to secure the planning and configuration flow of production. Before my arrival, product data and schedules were fragmented, managed manually, and prone to frequent entry errors.

  • Redesign the "product bible" into a single, consolidated reference database
  • Automate the weekly production scheduling and calculation of material requirements (MRP)
  • Develop an annual production load planning tool (CRP) adapted to local site constraints

🗂️ Mission 1 – Redesign and Automation of the Product Bible

The "product bible" is the reference database of all technical packaging specifications for the ALS site.

Cleaning and Consolidation

Using Python, I developed a script to merge three data sources (the old bible, weekly schedules, and operator control sheets). The program detected inconsistencies, intelligently merged duplicate fields, and exported critical anomalies for manual correction.

Business Rules Integration

  • Automatic sorting grid type association using keyword detection (e.g., detecting "brocoli")
  • Automated calculation of total theoretical bulk weight per pallet
  • Secure Excel input form for adding new products with consistency controls

Statistical Cadence Normalization

To make planning reliable, actual production cadences (measured in pallets/hour) showed high variability due to machine stops and breakdowns. I implemented a **robust statistical method excluding extreme deciles (lowest 10% and highest 10%)** using an R script.

This cleaned average is now used in planning to reflect standard production rates.

⚠️ Constraint encountered: Complete automation of palettisation configuration was discarded because many customers demand specific stacking layouts that do not follow general modelable rules.

📅 Mission 2 – Redesign and Automation of Scheduling & MRP

The weekly scheduling file is utilized to organize line work and anticipate necessary consumables.

Scheduling Optimization

I developed several VBA macros to improve the ergonomics of the packaging assistant's weekly workspace:

  • Automatic Sync: Real-time technical specification imports from the product bible upon opening the file.
  • Dynamic Formula Propagation: A macro scans and propagates search formulas across production blocks until the keyword "STOP" is reached.
  • Reset Button: One-click clearing of manually typed data from the previous week's schedule.

MRP (Material Requirement Planning) Automation

The MRP module automates comparisons between production plans and raw inventory files:

  • For films: Analyzes requirements, checks inventory, and references outstanding supplier orders.
  • For cartons: Automatically handles transitions between two carton reference codes (old and new references).
  • Highlights in red any consumable facing a stock-out.

📈 Mission 3 – Automation of the Annual CRP Tool

The CRP (Capacity Requirement Planning) tool allows management to anticipate the annual production load, identifying peaks or periods for maintenance.

The initial model file provided by the Ardo group was fully redesigned to fit ALS local constraints:

  • Load Balancing: Automatically distributes orders 50/50 between lines 1 and 2, which are technically identical.
  • Bulk Mixtures: Integrates bulk mixture products automatically into the capacity forecast of line 3.
  • Capacity Dashboard: Graphic visual tracking of the load compared to a critical warning threshold set at 80% capacity.
